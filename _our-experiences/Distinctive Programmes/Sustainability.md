<p><strong>Changkat Primary School Sustainability Framework</strong>
</p>
<p>At Changkat Primary, sustainability is a way of life. Guided by MOE’s
Eco Stewardship Programme, our <strong>GoGreen Plan</strong> nurtures students
to become environmentally responsible citizens. Our framework connects <strong>Curriculum, Campus, Culture, and Community (4Cs)</strong> with
three dimensions of learning: <strong>Learning About, Learning For, and Learning As sustainability.</strong>
</p>

<p><strong>Curriculum – Learning About Sustainability</strong>
</p>
<p><strong>Learning Through Experience</strong>
</p>
<p>Our students don’t just read about sustainability — they live it. In the <strong>Farm-to-Table Programme,</strong> Primary
5 students get their hands dirty (and sometimes wet!) as they try out three
different types of farming:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Hydroponics – growing vegetables without soil.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Aquaponics – combining fish and plants in a natural cycle.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Conventional farming – planting and harvesting the traditional way.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>Alongside, our Applied Learning Programme weaves in coding and project
work, helping students explore real-world environmental issues in creative
ways.</p>
<p></p>

<p><strong>Campus – Learning As Sustainability</strong>
</p>
<p><strong>A Living Classroom</strong>
</p>
<p>Our school is itself a teaching tool. The <strong>EcoHub</strong> is where
students see sustainability in action — from energy-saving ideas to upcycled
creations. In the <strong>wormery</strong>, they learn how even food scraps
can be turned into valuable fertiliser. Our <strong>Butterfly Trail and Science Garden</strong> give
them a chance to explore biodiversity right on campus.</p>
<p></p>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">
<img style="width: 100%" height="auto" width="100%" alt="" src="/images/OurExperiences/Sustainability/sus3.png">
</div>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>Campus – Learning As Sustainability</strong>
</p>
<p><strong>Building Habits That Last</strong>
</p>
<p>From recycling bins in every class to the Clean Plate Campaign in the
canteen, our students practice small, daily actions that add up to big
impact. At every level, they take part in activities like upcycling projects,
recycling drives, and social enterprise days, learning that caring for
the Earth is part of caring for each other.</p>
<p><strong>&nbsp;Community – Extending Sustainability Beyond School</strong>
</p>
<p><strong>Sharing With the Community</strong>
</p>
<p>Sustainability doesn’t stop at our school gate. Through the SustainaTrail@Changkat–Simei,
we welcome the community and partner schools to join us in celebrating
Earth Day and Go Green SG. Our students also connect with national partners
like NEA, PUB, and the Science Centre, gaining insights into Singapore’s
efforts to build a greener future.</p>
