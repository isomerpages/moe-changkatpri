<h3>&nbsp;</h3>
<h3><strong>Science @ Changkat Primary</strong></h3>
<hr>
<p><strong>Our Vision</strong>
</p>
<p><em>Nurturing Inquirers, Thinkers and Changemakers of a Sustainable Future</em>
</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>Our Mission</strong>
</p>
<p>To provide joyful, authentic, and inquiry-driven science experiences that
develop curiosity, critical thinking, collaboration, and responsible action
for a more sustainable world.</p>
<hr>
<p><strong>Our Science Philosophy</strong>
</p>
<p>At <strong>Changkat Primary</strong>, we believe that <strong>every child is naturally curious</strong>.
Guided by a <strong>student-centred approach</strong> and aligned with the <strong>2023 MOE Science Syllabus</strong>,
our programme nurtures learners who are:</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">
<img style="width: 100%" height="auto" width="100%" alt="" src="/images/OurExperiences/Curriculum/Sci/1.png">
</div>
<p></p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p><strong>Inquirers</strong>, who explore and question the world</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Critical thinkers</strong>, who reason with evidence</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Adaptive thinkers</strong>, who can flexibly adjust their ideas</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Custodians of the environment</strong>, who care for the world
they live in</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>Science learning in CKPS is <strong>authentic, meaningful, and rooted in real-world contexts</strong>.
We show students how Science is woven into daily life, helping them understand
how their actions impact the environment and the community around them.</p>
<hr>
<p><strong>Our Approach to Teaching &amp; Learning</strong>
</p>
<p>Learning Science at Changkat is grounded in <strong>inquiry-based learning</strong>,
hands-on discovery, and authentic experiences. Our curriculum emphasises:</p>
<p><strong>1. Authentic Real-World Contexts</strong>
</p>
<p>Students explore scientific concepts in meaningful settings — gardens,
trails, EcoHub, school grounds, and community spaces.</p>
<p>&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">
<img style="width: 100%" height="auto" width="100%" alt="" src="/images/OurExperiences/Curriculum/Sci/2.png">
</div>
<p><strong>2. Inquiry-Based Learning (5E Model)</strong>
</p>
<p>Using <strong>Engage, Explore, Explain, Elaborate, Evaluate</strong>, pupils
construct deep conceptual understanding through investigations and guided
discovery.</p>
<p><strong>3. Capture–Construct–Consolidate (3Cs)</strong>
</p>
<p>Our teachers design lessons that:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p><strong>Capture</strong> curiosity</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Construct</strong> understanding through activity and dialogue</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Consolidate</strong> learning through reflection</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>4. ICT-Enabled Learning</strong>
</p>
<p>Digital tools such as ClassPoint, Quizizz, and blogging platforms are
used to promote:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p><strong>Self-Directed Learning (SDL)</strong>
</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Collaborative Learning (CoL)</strong>
</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Real-time formative assessment</strong>
</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>5. Differentiated Learning</strong>
</p>
<p>Lesson packages from P3–P6 are designed based on students’ readiness and
learning profiles.</p>

<p><strong>Key Science Programmes &amp; Experiences</strong>
</p>
<p><strong>(1) Outdoor Experiential Learning</strong>
</p>
<p>The school grounds function as a <strong>living laboratory</strong>. Students
learn through:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p><strong>Butterfly Trail</strong>, where they care for caterpillars and
observe metamorphosis</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>EcoHub</strong>, where students explore topics like:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>recycling and food waste</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>composting (Wormery)</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>biodiversity and ecosystems</p>
</li>

<p>sustainable practices</p>
</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>

to sustainability.</p>

<p><strong>(4) Techniques for Tackling Science Questions</strong>
</p>
<p>To help pupils reason scientifically and articulate their ideas clearly,
CKPS teaches:</p>
<p><strong>Claim–Evidence–Reasoning (CER)</strong>
</p>
<p>For explanation and open-ended questions.</p>
<p><strong>STEPS Strategy</strong>
</p>
<p>For tackling question types in a structured, logical way.</p>
<p>These techniques are age-appropriate and progressive from P3–P6.
<br>Parents attend yearly workshops to support learning at home using the
same strategies.</p>
<hr>
<p><strong>Developing Critical &amp; Adaptive Thinkers</strong>
</p>
<p>Science learning at CKPS develops students who are able to:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>analyse evidence</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>evaluate observations</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>propose explanations</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>revise ideas based on new data</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>collaborate to solve problems</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>communicate scientifically and confidently</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>These are essential future-ready competencies that prepare them for a
rapidly changing world.</p>
